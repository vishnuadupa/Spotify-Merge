Everything Spotify won't let you do in one place.

A focused workspace for curating your library — not a player, a curator.

◎

See your whole library

Liked songs, playlists you own, and playlists you follow — every track pulled into one searchable list with album art and details.

⌘

Sort by language & genre

Songs grouped into language tabs, then filtered by genre — so your Telugu, Hindi, Korean and English tracks aren't all jumbled together.

⠿

Drag songs into playlists

A board of your playlists sits beside your library. Drag a song — or select many — and drop them straight into the playlist you want.

↑

Local-first, push when ready

Every edit is staged on your machine. Review it, undo anything, then push once. Matching playlists merge by name; new ones get created.

⇄

Move between accounts

Pull from one account and push to another — migrate your whole library across accounts, in either direction, no copy-paste.

◈

Visualize your taste

A mood map plots your songs so you can spot clusters and filter by feel — turn a vibe into a playlist in a couple of clicks.

Three steps, start to finish.

No accounts to create here, no data leaves your machine. You bring a Spotify app key; the app does the rest.

Connect

Paste your Spotify credentials once and connect your account. Connect a second account too if you're migrating.

Load your library

One click pulls in every song, playlist, and the data we can get — sorted into language and genre as it arrives.

Organize & push

Drag songs into playlists, tidy as long as you like, then push everything back to Spotify in one go.
